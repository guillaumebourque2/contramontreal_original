Klez Contra 2!

New England style contradances and Yiddish dances to music of Gypsies and Jews!

Christian Dawid

Berlin-based clarinet virtuoso, renowned for his groundbreaking work in Yiddish and other East-European styles.

" World-class clarinetist with a magically lyrical phrasing "

— Jüdische Allegemeine

Sergiu Popa

Sergiu, who began playing for weddings as a boy in his native Moldova with his father, a celebrated Gypsy accordionist, is now based in Montreal.

" A genius instrumentalist ... with one foot in the village wedding and the other exploring the orient. "

— Le Devoir

with Jessica Gal (violin), Rachel Lemisch (trombone), and Thierry Arsenault (percussion)
