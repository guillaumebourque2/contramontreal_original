GET THERE WITH GRACE


TIMELY AND CONSIDERATE DANCING TO DIFFERENT TUNES, FIGURES, AND TEMPOS

Notes for a workshop (playshop?) on style in contradancing

By Will Mentor

The set is more important than the individual.

1. Dance Safety

A. Elbows, Arms, and personal space in a crowded versus a roomy hall.
a. Swings (including twirling versus courtesy turn)(in a crowded hall those extended arms can be a club!)
b. Allemandes (steep W versus wide W)
c. Courtesy Turns (elbows are sharp)
d. Dancing in your space
e. Youth and adults in the same room
f. Personal space and comfort (swings and gypsies especially)

2. Dance Flow

A. Flow in a crowded hall versus a roomy hall at different tempos:
a. Swing to Men Allemande Left
b. Swing to Women Allemande Right or Ladies Chain
c. Swing to OL 1x versus Swing to OL 3/4 versus Swing to OL 3/4 Pass Thru
d. Walking into a swing
e. Ladies chain to forming a circle
f. Ladies chain to a star left
g. Star to star
h. Pausing on the ends of a hey
i. Dancing a courtesy turn

3. Being on Time

A. The set is more important than the individual! How to make sure you are where you need to be at different tempos!!
a. Ending the swing for: men allemande left, women allemande right, women's chain, circles, stars, everyone pulling by across, balance the ring
b. Circling left all the way around versus OL 3/4 versus OL 3/4 pass thru
c. Star to progress: star to star, star to balance new neighbor, full star to 3/4 star, full star to 7/8 star,
d. When to individually twirl in a do-si-do and when not to
e. What balances are asking you to do time-wise.
f. Did you get there too early?
